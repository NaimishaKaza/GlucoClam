## 🚀 How To Use GlucoCalm

### Step 1 — Onboarding
- Open the prototype link
- Enter your profile details: age, diabetes type, CGM device, and glucose target range
- Set your alert preferences — how and when you want to be notified

### Step 2 — Your Dashboard
- View your **real-time glucose levels** at a glance
- See trend arrows showing whether your glucose is rising, falling, or stable
- Get a daily summary of your time-in-range performance

### Step 3 — Smart Alerts
- GlucoCalm sends **intelligent, prioritized alerts** — not every single reading
- Alerts are categorized by urgency so you only get notified when it truly matters
- Reduce alarm fatigue while staying safe

### Step 4 — Glucose History & Trends
- Browse your **glucose history** by day, week, or month
- View pattern insights — identify what times of day your glucose spikes or dips
- Use trend data to have more informed conversations with your doctor

### Step 5 — Accessibility Features
- Switch between display modes designed for different visual needs
- Large text, high contrast, and simplified navigation built in throughout

## 💡 What Problem Does GlucoCalm Solve?

People living with diabetes who use Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) face a hidden crisis: 
**alarm fatigue**. CGM devices can fire dozens of alerts per day — many of them unnecessary — 
causing users to become desensitized and start ignoring alarms, including critical ones.

Existing CGM apps also fail users with accessibility needs, and their interfaces are cluttered 
and anxiety-inducing rather than calming and supportive.

GlucoCalm reimagines the CGM experience with intelligent alert prioritization, 
accessibility-first design, and a calm, clear interface that puts the user in control.

## 👤 Who Is GlucoCalm For?

- **People with Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes** using a CGM device
- **Users experiencing alarm fatigue** from their current CGM app
- **Older adults or users with visual impairments** who need accessible health interfaces
- **Caregivers** monitoring a family member's glucose levels remotely
- **Clinicians** who want patients to arrive with better data and insights

## 🔄 Product Lifecycle

1. **Research** — Identified alarm fatigue and accessibility gaps across 3 competitor CGM products
2. **Define** — Translated user research into a prioritized requirements backlog
3. **Design** — Built v1 prototype in Figma based on requirements
4. **Test** — Conducted task-based usability testing with real users
5. **Iterate** — Refined to v2 based on test findings, improving task success from baseline to 91.6%
6. **Evaluate** — Used Claude and ChatGPT to run AI-assisted heuristic evaluation against Nielsen's 10 heuristics extended with FDA use-safety principles
